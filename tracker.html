How this works

Set a module's Status to Done, then fill in the Actual Done date and Actual Days you spent. The page recomputes the +/− vs target for that module and the headline runway number. Everything saves in this browser automatically — no account, no server.

The headline reads from your most recent finish: positive means days of buffer still in hand before 12 Nov, negative means you'll overrun by that many days.

Kill switch

If the runway goes negative and you can't claw it back inside a week, cut Wi-Fi, not Web. CWEE is the hardest to rush and the most valuable to your offensive-security brand; CWPE is the shortest and most self-contained.

Two structural risks the dates can't fix: the flat 6-sections/day pace treats a CWEE whitebox exploit-dev section like an AI reading — it isn't, so guard the buffer. And this whole schedule assumes a 16 Jun start with CAPE already cleared off your plate.
